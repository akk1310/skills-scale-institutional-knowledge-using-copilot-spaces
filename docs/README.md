## OctoAcme Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme employs a structured, iterative project management methodology grounded in customer value delivery and clear ownership. Our approach balances strategic planning with agile execution, emphasizing data-driven decisions and psychological safety. Every project is governed by five core principles: customer-first prioritization, iterative delivery in small testable increments, clear role ownership with designated Project Managers and Product Leads, evidence-based decision making, and an environment where team members feel safe sharing feedback and learning from challenges.

Our organizational structure defines key personas—Project Managers who coordinate delivery and manage schedules and risks, Product Managers who define outcomes and prioritize work, Developers who implement features and collaborate on design, QA/Testing teams who validate quality, and Stakeholders who provide inputs and approvals. Communication flows through a clear cadence: weekly syncs between PM and Product Manager, twice-weekly standups for delivery teams, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations as needed. This ensures transparency and alignment across all levels.

The project lifecycle follows a predictable five-phase progression: **Initiation** establishes the problem statement and stakeholders; **Planning** scopes work, allocates resources, and identifies dependencies; **Execution** focuses on building, testing, and iterating; **Release** handles deployment and verification; and **Close & Retrospective** captures learnings for continuous improvement. Throughout execution, we maintain robust risk management by identifying risks early, assessing their impact and likelihood, implementing mitigation strategies, and reviewing status at weekly syncs. Our quality assurance practices are comprehensive—combining unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, security scanning, and manual QA—ensuring reliable, maintainable code reaches production.

Critical to OctoAcme's success is our emphasis on structured communication and transparent escalation paths. We use standardized templates for weekly status updates, incident communication, and decision logging. Blocker escalation follows a three-level model: team-level triage in daily standups, PM escalation to Product Leadership and dependent teams, and sponsor-level escalation for business-impacting issues. By centralizing institutional knowledge in shared documentation, versioning processes, and maintaining decision logs, we accelerate onboarding, reduce single-person dependency risk, and enable consistent, repeatable project execution across the organization.

## Contributing to OctoAcme Project Management Docs

We continuously refine and improve our processes based on team feedback and evolving needs. To contribute:

1. **Identify gaps or improvements** — If you notice missing guidance or outdated processes, open an issue.
2. **Update docs collaboratively** — Submit pull requests with proposed changes. Tag relevant stakeholders for review.
3. **Use a Copilot Space** — Add these docs to a Copilot Space (`.copilot/` folder) for context-aware assistance during projects.
4. **Version & link** — Keep cross-references current and document the rationale for process changes in commit messages.
